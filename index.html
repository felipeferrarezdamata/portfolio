About Me

I design for the moment after training ends.

My approach to instructional design starts with a single question: what do people need to be able to do differently? Not what content to cover — what behaviour to shift. That distinction shapes everything from how I write learning objectives to how I design assessments.

I have spent the past several years working as an L&D Consultant at Langara College and Douglas College, designing and delivering programs in HR Management, Organizational Behaviour, and Business Strategy for adult learners. I design end-to-end — needs analysis, objectives, content, delivery, evaluation — and I treat every iteration as a data point.

My technical toolkit includes Articulate Rise (active proficiency), Articulate Storyline (currently building branching scenario capability), Vyond / Synthesia (AI video for async delivery), and Notion for knowledge management and content architecture. I apply AI tools — including Claude, Microsoft Copilot, and Gamma AI — to accelerate design cycles without sacrificing depth.

I hold a Train the Trainer Certificate from Vancouver Community College, am completing the BC Provincial Instructor Diploma Program (PIDP), and hold two graduate degrees including an MBA. I am based in Burnaby, BC and available immediately.

Workplace Conduct & Regulatory Compliance
Ensuring all employees and managers understand their rights, responsibilities, and obligations under BC employment and human rights legislation.
Program Rationale

Compliance training is often treated as a checkbox exercise. This program is designed differently: content is grounded in realistic workplace scenarios rather than abstract policy summaries, and assessment measures applied understanding rather than recall. The goal is not just completion — it is confidence to act correctly when a real situation arises.

The program is structured for a mixed audience of individual contributors and people leaders, with differentiated scenarios reflecting each group's distinct responsibilities under BC's Human Rights Code, WorkSafeBC regulations, and the Employment Standards Act.

Terminal Learning Objectives
1
Identify prohibited grounds of discrimination under BC's Human Rights Code and explain organizational obligations
2
Recognize behaviours that constitute workplace harassment and distinguish them from acceptable workplace conduct
3
Apply the duty to accommodate principle to common workplace scenarios involving disability, religion, and family status
4
Follow the correct incident reporting procedure when witnessing or experiencing a policy violation
5
Describe the organization's investigation process and the protections available to complainants and witnesses
6
Demonstrate manager-specific obligations under the duty to investigate and maintain a harassment-free workplace

Assessment & Compliance Architecture
Per-module — Formative
Knowledge Checks
3–5 questions per module tied to specific regulatory provisions. Minimum 80% pass rate required to advance. Unlimited attempts with feedback on each response.
Program Completion — Summative
Final Attestation
Learner attestation confirming they have read, understood, and will comply with organizational policies. Timestamped and stored in LMS for audit purposes.
Manager Pathway — Extended
Applied Scenarios
Additional branching scenarios for people leaders covering investigation obligations, accommodation requests, and reporting responsibilities. Separate completion record.
Design Notes

Regulatory accuracy: All content references the BC Human Rights Code, WorkSafeBC OHS regulations, and the Employment Standards Act. Program includes a review trigger — any legislative update prompts a content review within 30 days of the change taking effect.

Scenario sourcing: Scenarios are based on anonymized patterns from BC Human Rights Tribunal decisions, making the content feel real rather than hypothetical and building recognition of what actual violations look like in practice.

Accessibility: All modules designed to WCAG 2.1 AA standards — closed captions on all video, alt text on all images, keyboard-navigable throughout. Available in English with French translation support documented for future iteration.
